Exciting Event: Honeypottin' Hitler

On a particularly wintery evening in Krakow, two men stumble down a cobblestone street. They are completely loaded: the condensation in their exhaled breath is 99% wine - when they kiss their wives good morning in a few hours they will still taste of wine. Possibly the taste of wine will never leave their mouths. It's not really any of our business.

The street is almost perfectly dark: moonlight reflects off the raw sewage which has collected in the gutters, the smoke from dying fireplaces across the city curls into the night sky. One of the men has tied his shabby coat around his neck like a cape and prances down the street while his companion uses a wine bottle to blow out a fair approximation of a folk jig.

The Ghetto is not typically a place for celebration, but these two know something the rest of the district doesn't. As it happens, you don't know about it either, so let's go back in time a few weeks.

Late on a Tuesday night, a girl is dying her hair blond in the kitchen sink. She uses a combination of old fertiliser and vanilla essence, a mixture that she traded for three pairs of socks and half an apple. Perched on stools, five men in battered suits survey the situation dubiously. One is holding a book upside down and pretending to read while another smokes nervously, ashing into a chipped mug.

She asks: what do you think?

A woman making gruel by the stove, looks up: it will look better when it's curled.
